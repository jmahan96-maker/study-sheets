NEUROANATOMY OVERVIEW

BRIEFING DOCUMENT

⭐ HIGH-YIELD CRNA BOARD SUMMARY

Most testable facts for CRNA boards — review before exam.

TOPIC HIGH-YIELD FACT
Neuron basics Basic functional unit; cell body (soma) + dendrites (receive) + axons (conduct); longest cells in body; LIMITED repair ability after injury
Spinal cord termination Conus medullaris terminates at L1–L2 in adults; below = cauda equina
Cauda equina Roots below L1–L2 that must descend through vertebral canal before exiting; basis for spinal anesthesia safety below L2
Spinal nerve count 31 pairs total: 8 cervical / 12 thoracic / 5 lumbar / 5 sacral / 1 coccygeal
8 cervical nerves 7 cervical vertebrae but 8 cervical nerves — C1 exits ABOVE C1 vertebra
Dermatome vs. myotome Dermatome = sensory via DORSAL root; myotome = motor via VENTRAL root; C1 has NO sensory dermatome
Blood-nerve barrier Perineurium (squamous epithelial sheath around fascicles) = blood-nerve barrier
Connective tissue layers Endoneurium (each axon) → Perineurium (fascicle) → Epineurium (whole nerve)
Fascicle anatomy NOT continuous — divide & anastomose throughout nerve; explains nerve injury patterns & intraneural injection risks
DRG location Dorsal Root Ganglion: sensory cell bodies OUTSIDE spinal cord on dorsal root
Sensory vs. motor roots DORSAL = sensory (afferent ARRIVES); VENTRAL = motor (efferent EXITS) — mnemonic: DAVE (Dorsal Afferent, Ventral Efferent)
Rami Both dorsal & ventral rami carry BOTH sensory & motor fibers; ALL nerve plexuses are on VENTRAL rami
Phrenic nerve C3–C5 ("C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive"); part of cervical plexus
Brachial plexus roots C5–T1 (ventral rami); innervates entire upper extremity
Lumbar plexus roots L1–L3 + upper L4 (±T12 in ~50%); key branches: femoral, obturator, lateral femoral cutaneous, ilioinguinal
Sacral plexus roots Lumbosacral trunk (L4–L5) + S1–S4; Sciatic nerve (L4–S2) → common peroneal + tibial; Pudendal nerve (S2–S4)
Sympathetic ('thoracolumbar') T1–L2; preganglionic → white rami communicans → sympathetic trunk (paravertebral ganglia adjacent to vertebral bodies) → gray rami → destination
Parasympathetic ('craniosacral') Cranial nerves + S2–S4; S2–S4 via ventral roots → ventral rami
Ganglion definition Collection of cell bodies OUTSIDE the spinal cord (vs. nucleus = cell bodies INSIDE CNS)
Interneurons Responsible for reflexes (from presenter notes)
Intraneural injection risk Fascicle anatomy (anastomotic, non-continuous) makes intraneural injection especially dangerous
